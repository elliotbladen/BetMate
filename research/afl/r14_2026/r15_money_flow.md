## TEAM-BY-TEAM MONEY FLOW ANALYSIS

---

### 1. ADELAIDE CROWS — PUBLIC WILL OVER-BET THEM

**R14 result:** Won by 57 (121-64 vs Bulldogs)
**ESPN narrative:** "The Crows are back to being premiership contenders" — this headline will drive massive public action on Adelaide for R15.

**Why to fade the public move:**
- The 57-point win was largely delivered by a **nine-goal first quarter** — an outlier performance that is statistically very unlikely to repeat
- Adelaide started this season 1-3. They've now won 8 of their last 11, which is genuinely impressive, BUT context: they've had relatively soft scheduling post-bye
- Their R15 opponent is **Carlton** — a physical, contested-ball team that will make life harder than the Bulldogs did
- The Bulldogs had key players carrying niggles — Adelaide feasted on a below-strength opponent
- **Oskar Pedlar (hamstring) misses R15** — he was a key cog in the midfield engine that drove the first quarter blitz

**Betting signal:** Adelaide will be priced ~$1.60-$1.70 and may deserve to be $1.80-$1.90 given Carlton's defensive structure and Pedlar's absence. **Don't chase the Crows at short price.**

**If value exists:** Carlton at any price above $2.20 is worth examining. They come off a bye (rested, fresh), and their contested ball strength will trouble Adelaide's structure.

### 2. SYDNEY SWANS — MARKET WILL OVER-SHORTEN THEM, THEY'RE NOT AS GOOD AS 12-2 LOOKS

**R14 result:** Won by 3 (93-90 vs Port Adelaide) — trailed by 9 at three-quarter time
**Narrative:** "Swans join Fremantle at top of the ladder" — equal first, irresistible public narrative

**Why to fade:**
- Sydney were comprehensively outplayed for three quarters by a team (Port) that sits 12th on the ladder with four wins
- Port's first-half inaccuracy (7 behinds) inflated Sydney's position — if Port kicks at average, this is a 20-point loss for Sydney
- **Tom McCartin (concussion) and Justin McInerney (hamstring, 8+ weeks) miss R15** — Sydney's defensive structure takes a significant hit
- Sydney's R15 opponent is **Gold Coast** — historically friendly matchup, so the public will pile on Sydney at short odds
- The pattern of "scraping home" (Sydney have won multiple games in Q4 this year) eventually catches up. This was their second time coming from behind in Q4 this month.

**Betting signal:** Sydney will be $1.40-$1.50 vs Gold Coast. With two key defenders out and after a draining come-from-behind win, the line (handicap) is where value lies — take Gold Coast +15.5 or more if available.

### 3. PORT ADELAIDE — MARKET WILL OVER-PUNISH THEM FOR THE LOSS

**R14 result:** Lost by 3 (90-93 vs Sydney) — led by 9 at three-quarter time
**Narrative:** "Power blown out by Swans in final quarter" — public will remember only the loss

**Why to back Port (or at least not fade them):**
- Port's statistical performance was BETTER than Sydney's for three quarters
- They had 25 scoring shots to Sydney's 23 — Port lost despite having more opportunities
- The loss was entirely due to shooting accuracy, not structural issues
- R15 opponent is **Collingwood** (MCG) — Pies returning from bye, which creates a pricing complexity
- Port (4-10) sit 12th but their underlying performance metrics are better than their record suggests — unlucky shooting round-to-round

**Betting signal:** Port at inflated odds vs Collingwood deserve attention. Expect them to open $2.80-$3.10 due to the loss narrative, but Collingwood coming off bye carry their own risk (see below).

### 4. COLLINGWOOD — BYE-BOUNCE NARRATIVE VS GENUINE READINESS

**R14:** Had bye
**R15:** vs Port Adelaide at MCG

**Context:** Teams returning from bye historically perform well (AFL research shows +3-4% win rate from bye bounce). But Collingwood's 2026 form has been inconsistent — they're not in the top four conversation. The MCG is their home ground which adds +1-2 points to their line.

**Betting signal:** Collingwood will be ~$1.65-$1.75 vs Port. Their bye advantage is real, but Port's underlying performance vs Sydney shows more quality than 4 wins suggests. Market may be over-generous to Collingwood.

### 5. HAWTHORN — BYE-BOUNCE + OPPONENT CONTEXT

**R14:** Had bye (sitting at or near top four)
**R15:** vs Western Bulldogs

**Context:** Hawks are premiership favourites ($7.50 in the market), coming off a bye. Bulldogs just got demolished by 57 points — the public will hammer Hawthorn at short odds.

**Why to be cautious:**
- The Bulldogs' 57-point loss was against Adelaide at peak Q1 performance — it was a specific opponent-driven anomaly, not a general structural collapse
- Hawthorn at home vs Bulldogs is a different proposition — Bulldogs have shown genuine top-four form across the season
- Bontempelli (captain, elite midfielder) was held quiet vs Adelaide but will demand more in this matchup

**Betting signal:** If Hawthorn opens $1.50 or shorter vs Bulldogs, the Bulldogs handicap (+14.5 or more) is the bet. Don't read the 57-point loss as proof the Bulldogs are bad — it was an Adelaide-specific outlier.

### 7. BRISBANE LIONS — MARKET LOVES THEM, BUT THEY ONLY LED BY 3 AT HALF-TIME VS RICHMOND

**R14 result:** Won by 35 (115-80 vs Richmond) — led by 3 at half-time
**R15:** vs North Melbourne

**Narrative problem:** The 35-point win sounds dominant. But Brisbane trailed in Q1 and only led by three at half-time against a team (Richmond, 2-12) that has lost 12 games this season.

**Why to be cautious:**
- The Q3 blitz (7 goals to 3) came against a Richmond team that had burned its legs trying to keep pace in the first half
- Will Ashcroft's 38 disposals were excellent, but even he was quiet in the first half
- North Melbourne (6-8) will be highly motivated after their thrilling one-point win over West Coast — emotional high
- North's contested ball (119 vs 116 vs West Coast) showed genuine competitiveness
- Harry Sheezel (32 disp, 9 clearances vs West Coast) is the best young player in the competition — he'll demand respect

**Betting signal:** Brisbane should be $1.60-$1.70 vs North. Don't expect Brisbane to mirror their R14 Q3 performance — North are a better team than Richmond by significant margin. North Melbourne at $2.40-$2.60 is worth a look for punters willing to take a risk on their emotional bounce.

## KEY PRINCIPLES FOR THIS ROUND

1. **Bye bounce is real but can be overpriced.** Hawthorn, Collingwood, Carlton, Fremantle all coming off byes — the market adjusts but doesn't always get the magnitude right.

2. **Single-quarter outliers don't persist.** Adelaide's nine-goal Q1 is a statistical outlier. Markets will over-price them in R15 based on that one performance.

3. **Scoreline vs statistical truth.** Port Adelaide's loss, West Coast's loss, and Brisbane's "dominant win" all mask the underlying reality. Bet the stats, not the scoreboard.

4. **Injury list of GWS.** Tom Green (ACL), Toby Greene (test), Coniglio (hamstring) — any team facing GWS in R15 should be getting better odds than they appear. GWS could be severely depleted.

5. **Sydney's defensive losses.** McCartin and McInerney out — Sydney's defensive numbers will be worse in R15 than they look based on the 12-2 record. Gold Coast +handicap has value.
